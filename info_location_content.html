The tranquil heart of France

The Limousin is in the heart of France, in the foothills of the Massif Central, and is a mainly rural and agricultural area. It is close to the Dordogne with the caves (Grottes de Villars) and Cro Magnum sites, and the Perigord Vert which is famous for its gastronomy - foie gras, truffles, duck and Limousin beef. In the Autumn there are chestnut and wild-mushroom festivals

The Haute-Vienne is one of the departments of the Limousin, and is the least industrialised area of France. It is renowned for it's tranquil undisturbed, open rural countryside, and is rich in wildlife. It boasts 8 major lakes, 26 chateaux (Montbrun, Jumilhac), 2 theme parks many museums, woodland, and Heritage trails, pretty unspoiled villages, markets and festivals.

Accomodation

The hotel provides an excellent base to explore the Limousin, Dordogne, and Auvergne. The accommodation comprises a dozen en-suite chalet-type rooms, with a bar, restaurant, 13th century chapel, and several "special" fountains, in the middle of a forest with wonderful views in all directions. Special diets can be catered for.

The location is only a few kilometres from the border between the Limousin and Acquitaine. This boundary marks a very important historical border between an old part of England and the rest of France. This area is littered with castles which used to guard the border, like the spectacular Chateau Montbrun.

On the pilgrimage route to Santiago de Compostela - La Route de Saint Jacques

This area of the Limousin is on the main route south for Pilgrims to Santiago de Compostela, in North-West Spain. The emblem of the Pilgrims was the Scallop - or St Jacques (in French). You will see brass plaques on the pavements in many local towns showing the routes that the Pilgrims used to take.

Just south of Limoges

Limoges is the capital of the Limousin, and dates back to Roman times. It is famous for its porcelain, which is made from the local kaolin that is quarried near St. Yrieix-la-Perche, just south of Limoges, and a few kilometres from our base.
